The Pentium Pro Processor Meets MMX Technology

With the new Bravo LC 6233, AST continues its tradition of being first-to-market with leading-edge technology. The Intel Pentium II processor is Intel's fastest processor to date, combining the power of the Pentium Pro processor, with the capabilities of Intel's MMX media enhancement technology, giving business users the power to handle the most compute-intensive business applications.

The Pentium II processor also utilizes an innovative packaging technology called Single Edge Contact (S.E.C.) Cartridge which replaces Pin Grid Array (PGA) technology used on previous Intel processors. The most notable change to the Pentium II chip is that the level two cache and processors are integrated on a card, not a chip as in the Pentium Pro. With S.E.C., the Bravo LC 6233 is able to take advantage of future planned upgrades to Intel Pentium II processors, while remaining compatible with applications running on previous members of the Intel microprocessor family.

"AST is helping users in small and medium-size business to more efficiently and effectively drive business computing by combining their legacy of superior computing solutions with the latest Intel Pentium II processor," stated Paul S. Otellini, executive vice president, Sales and Marketing Group at Intel. "This combination will provide these users with the performance and flexibility to meet their computing needs."
