Executive summary:

"Nile" will address the commercial desktop entry level market segment and will be marketed under the Bravo EL name on a worldwide basis. "Nile" systems will be introduced in Q1’98 and will be direct replacements for the current shipping Bravo LC (Trixie) systems. These systems will represent the most price sensitive offerings from AST in the reseller channel. They will implement existing technologies with some performance enhancements, and an emphasis placed on aggressive pricing.

Positioning statement:

"The optimum solution for low-cost business computing"

Product Positioning

Key positioning points of "Nile" are:

  • Entry-level, non-multimedia commercial desktop platform
  • Quality, stable technologies in a desktop platform powerful enough to run today’s wide range of office suites as well as provide headroom for tomorrow’s new applications.

Target Market

Key market segments are:

  • Small/medium businesses
  • Bid opportunities
  • Local/State Government
  • Designed for customers primarily focused on value, but still concerned with desktop performance

 

Target Applications

  • Office suites (i.e. Excel/Word/Powerpoint)
  • E-mail / Communication apps
  • Database applications

Key Selling Points

  • Aggressive pricing
  • New ID (new bezel) with New "easy-maintainance" AST ATX chassis
  • Pentium II introduction to segment 0
  • Mainstream commercial desktop (no audio, no NIC)
  • SNR / UMS

Specification Highlights

  • PII 233/266/300 MHz CPU
  • Intel 440LX Core logic
  • 3.2/4.3GB UDMA-33 HDD
  • 24X (selected models)
  • SNR
  • UMS (WfM, DMI 2.0, LDCM 3.1)

Product Lifecycle

  • Q1’98 through Q4’98
  • FCS 2/13/98
  • LCS 12/31/98
