AST® COMPUTER MEETS CUSTOMER NEEDS FOR EASE AND PRODUCTIVITY
BY PRELOADING ITS SYSTEMS WITH IBM SPEECH RECOGNITION SOFTWARE

Bravo Commercial Desktop Users Start Talking to Their Computers

IRVINE, Calif., March 17, 1997 -- AST® Computer (NASDAQ: ASTA) and IBM (NYSE: IBM) today announced that AST Bravo PC users will soon have the power of speech recognition technology on their desktops. The first non-IBM manufacturer to equip its systems with VoiceType 3.0, AST will preload its Bravo commercial desktop computers with IBM's innovative software. Continuing its strategy of being first-to-market with leading-edge technology, AST is scheduled to begin shipments of the speech-enabled Bravo in the second quarter.

"Our corporate users are hungry for technologies that allow them to efficiently and effectively multi-task in an ever-changing demanding environment," stated Laura Raybin, director, brand marketing, AST Computer. "By preloading our Bravo systems with voice recognition software, we are equipping our customers with another tool to increase their office productivity."

Without even touching a keyboard or mouse, Bravo PC users will be able to open applications, dictate memos, surf the Internet and send e-mail messages, simply by speaking to their computers. VoiceType 3.0 for Microsoft® Windows® 95 allows people to dictate 70-100 words per minute at an average accuracy rate of 90 percent to help corporate users become more productive. Its advanced features include speaker independence so that people do not have to train their computers to their individual voice patterns. The software's adaptive learning capability means that the accuracy of word and accent recognition improves with use.

"We are very happy that AST has chosen VoiceType to provide their customers with an exciting technology that will transform the way they work," said Jan Winston, worldwide speech systems manager. "The preload of VoiceType on the AST commercial computer line-up will allow professionals to readily experience the benefits of speech technology, such as productivity and ease of use."

The VoiceType software on AST's award-winning Bravo PCs will support a number of languages, including American and UK English, Spanish, German, Italian, French and Japanese.

Speech Recognition Leadership

IBM set the industry standard for speech recognition software when it launched the VoiceType family of products. With more than 25 years of research in the field, IBM has built a team of speech recognition specialists worldwide. The VoiceType family of products includes VoiceType 3.0 for Windows 95, VoiceType Dictation 1.32 for Windows 3.x and Windows 95, VoiceType Control 3.0, VoiceType Developers Toolkit Version 3.0 for Windows 95 and OS/2® Warp 4, which includes VoiceType.
