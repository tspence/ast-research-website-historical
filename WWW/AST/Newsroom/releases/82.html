AST® COMPUTER BRINGS AUDIO AND VISUAL COMPUTING TECHNOLOGIES TO LIFE AT COMDEX

LAS VEGAS, Nov. 11, 1996 -- All eyes and ears will be on AST Computer (NASDAQ:ASTA) at Comdex/Fall '96 as they showcase one of the most expansive displays of computing technologies and products. Highlighting AST's product demonstrations will be the debut of their newest line of Manhattan™ servers. For new technology enthusiasts, AST is showcasing DVD technology and the Intel Pentium® Processor with MMX™ Technology, and is conducting a live broadcast feed from the show floor directly to AST's home page. In addition, the company will display their complete line of Ascentia™ notebooks, Manhattan servers, Advantage!® consumer PCs and Bravo commercial desktops. AST's award-winning products will appear in booth #L2612 at the Las Vegas Convention Center, Nov. 18-22.

"AST will have a bigger presence than ever at this year's Comdex. Sights, sounds and new technologies will be just the beginning," stated Dan Sheppard, vice president, global corporate marketing at AST. "Our new product introductions and technology demonstrations illustrate AST's working commitment to offer customers next generation technology at truly aggressive prices."

Feeding Your PC
AST brings Comdex right to your PC by broadcasting a unique live feed from the AST booth directly to AST's Home Page. For those who want to see what's happening on the show floor minute-by-minute, they can access AST's innovative Home Page at http://www.ast.com. With three cameras and live audio feed, it will feel as if you were right there - all from the comfort of your own PC.

For those editors attending Comdex, AST will have a dedicated PC running in the AST suite, N235 located on the second level of the Convention Center. Instead of getting loaded down with press information, editors can download just the information they need or they can email it directly to their own PC.

AST Announces New Manhattan Servers
Debuting at Comdex/Fall '96 will be the Manhattan D Series and Manhattan Presence™ Pro entry-level servers. Announced today, these servers compliment AST's line of high-performance, industry-leading server solutions. Available in two configurations, the Manhattan D Series is a high-performance file and print or workgroup server and the Manhattan Presence Pro is a complete Internet/Intranet server solution. These new Manhattan servers are offered at industry-leading prices.

Key hardware features of the Manhattan D Series and the Manhattan Presence Pro include a 200MHz Intel® Pentium® Pro processor, Intel 440FX chipset, 32MB ECC EDO RAM expandable to 512MB, integrated PCI Ultra Wide SCSI, ten drive bays, integrated 10/100 MB Fast Ethernet and Intel LANDesk® Server Manager for comprehensive system management.

Combining all the best hardware of the Manhattan D Series with leading Internet software, the Manhattan Presence Pro is one of the most complete Internet servers on the market. Among its many features, the Manhattan Presence Pro comes pre-loaded with Web server software, Web authoring, E-mail and search engines - everything needed to easily get a business up and running on the Web.

AST Showcases DVD and Intel® Pentium® Processor with MMX™ Technology
Visitors to AST's booth can experience DVD technology and the Intel Pentium Processor with MMX™ Technology in action on AST's mobile and desktop computers. MMX Technology, Intel's newest addition to the Pentium processor range, is designed to speed up multimedia applications such as video-conferencing, sound and 3-D graphics.

One of the hottest emerging CD technologies today, DVD, is also showcased on an AST consumer desktop computer. Among its many features, DVD provides the capability to view full-length, home theater-quality movies from your PC, with one double-sided disc providing up to 18GB of storage capacity. Sight, sound, performance and capacity all make DVD one of the leaders in new media communications technologies.

Mobile Computing with AST
AST's complete line of Ascentia notebook computers will be on display, including the Ascentia J55 notebook and the recently announced Ascentia P50. The highly acclaimed Ascentia P50 now has a larger display, 133MHz Intel Pentium processor, 6X CD-ROM drive, integrated 28.8 Kbps fax/data modem and 24MB of standard base memory. The Ascentia J55 is the latest addition to the award-winning Ascentia J product line, and features an Intel Pentium 133MHz processor, 11.3-inch active matrix SVGA color display, 33.6 Kbps data/fax modem and 16MB of standard RAM.

AST is also showing the Ascentia A Series geared for the retail market. The Ascentia A provides the right features at the right price. Equipped with a powerful 120MHz Intel Pentium processor, 6X CD-ROM, a 12.1-inch or 11.3-inch color display and a host of extras, it is ideal for home users or for value-oriented corporate customers who want to defy obsolescence and protect their computing investment.

Advantage! Consumer Desktops
AST takes center stage with the Advantage! line of award-winning feature-rich desktop computers. Designed as solutions to meet a diverse set of interests and lifestyles, the Advantage! consumer personal desktops are targeted at four market segments: Home Office, Communications, Gaming and Education, as well as general consumer computing.

The Advantage! line-up encompasses the most advanced technologies available featuring everything from interactive communications and high-resolution 3-D graphics and sound to full-page scanners and infrared remote capabilities.

Show attendees can experience the latest in video-conferencing on the Advantage! 9312 Communications system that will be demonstrated daily at the AST booth.

Bravo Commercial Desktops
Rounding-out AST's complete line of computing solutions is the Bravo series of commercial desktops. Equipped with Windows NT 4.0, the Bravo MS-T 6180 and 6200 desktops address the needs of corporate users looking to make the transition to Windows NT Workstation and Pentium Pro processor technology. Packed with Intel's fastest Pentium Pro 180 and 200MHz processor engines and Intel's 440FX PCI set, the Bravo line enables users to take advantage of today's movement into 32-bit environments.
