AST® COMPUTER DELIVERS GOLDEN SILENCE
WITH NEW COMMERCIAL DESKTOPS

AST Continues Its Leadership In "Green PC" Technology By Responding
To Business Users' Need For A Quiet Workplace With The Bravo MS:
The First Silent Business PC In The U.S.

IRVINE, Calif., May 27, 1997 - Silently conquering office noise pollution, AST® Computer today whispered that its latest Bravo MS commercial desktop computers are the first U.S. commercial PCs to be equipped with System Noise Reduction (SNR) technology. Dramatically reducing the dissonant noise that resounds through every office, the new Bravo MS computer line is as much as 70 percent quieter than other leading business PCs.

"We've consulted with our corporate customers and responded with business solutions that help their employees work better and faster," said Laura Raybin, AST director, brand marketing. "Our Bravo MS systems are packed with leading-edge hardware and software, in a package that's conducive to maximum office efficiency. This innovative technology is truly ground breaking."

After its successful launch into the European market, AST brings SNR technology to U.S. businesses for the first time. AST's quiet Bravo PCs give users relief from noise distractions when concentrating on important projects or engaging in conference calls. In large offices with numerous computers running simultaneously, SNR technology creates a significantly more pleasant and productive work environment.

"With the commercial market growing more competitive, there is growing attention among PC vendors to address environmental concerns in the workplace," said Kevin Hause, analyst, personal systems research, International Data Corporation. "Reducing system noise levels can have a potential for a positive impact on workplace productivity."

Silencing a Noisy Problem

AST breaks new ground with its latest Bravo MS commercial desktops by introducing the industry's first "Silent PC." Building upon its roots as a pioneer in "Green PC" technology, AST is the first manufacturer to equip its desktop computers with SNR technology. First introduced in Europe by AST, SNR tackles office noise pollution head-on - reducing system noise by as much as 70 percent as compared to standard PCs. A huge relief to offices with numerous computers operating simultaneously, SNR is also helpful when engaged in conference calls, training sessions or when conducting research.

System noise reduction is one of the biggest advances in computer design in years. By incorporating a noise-dampening enclosure, the mechanical noise emitted from the hard drive is reduced. A variable speed fan inside the power supply keeps the system cool, while operating quietly. Now, companies have an insurance policy against 'white noise pollution.'

Surpassing Standards for Silence

A pioneer in environmentally-friendly computing with its 1993 award-winning Bravo LP "Green PC" line, AST continues its leadership role by being the first PC manufacturer to take to heart the National Institute for Occupational Safety and Health's (NIOSH) "Buy Quiet" policy for new equipment purchases. Exceeding the NIOSH guidelines for office noise exposure, AST's Bravo MS runs at 28dB - 57dB quieter than the recommended exposure limit for office equipment.

According to the Bureau of Census data, an estimated 30 million American workers are 'noise exposed' on a daily basis. For example, the noise generated from one PC can generate 35-40 decibels - almost the level of a normal conversation. Imagine what this can mean with a room full of PCs! More businesses are beginning to recognize that noise pollution does indeed impact productivity and have adopted guidelines for selecting new computers through this "Buy Quiet" policy. The New AST Bravo MS systems allow corporate users to meet these guidelines today.

Pricing and Availability

With expected street prices starting at $1,795, Bravo MS systems equipped with SNR technology will be available in May.
