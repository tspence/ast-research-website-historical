AST TO SUPPORT INTEL MMX™ TECHNOLOGY IN FUTURE PRODUCT DEVELOPMENTS

New Technology Will Enable A Host of New and Improved Multimedia PC Applications

IRVINE, Calif., March 5, 1996 -- Continuing to pursue innovative and leading-edge technology solutions, AST Computer today announced its plans for support of Intel's major architecture enhancement, designated MMX™ technology, that is designed to provide fast, powerful and cost-effective multimedia solutions for use in a host of applications.

MMX, the next generation Intel architecture, incorporates new instructions and datatypes to accelerate multimedia and communication applications for faster graphics, faster video, faster picture processing and faster music synthesis.

"AST is proud to announce its support and commitment to Intel's new MMX technology," said Ian Diery, AST's president and chief executive officer. "We believe that this technology is great news for future developments of advanced multimedia systems. Combining AST's expertise in process design with the most significant addition to the Intel Architecture instruction set since the Intel386™ processor, reinforces AST's efforts to partner with industry champions and deliver leading-edge technology solutions to our customers."

AST believes that MMX technology will be embraced by graphics, audio and modem vendors. It is expected to provide a strong source of computing power for new communication and multimedia applications and will also provide better system price performance.

AST plans to target several areas for MMX technology including video conferencing, MPEG 2 video and DSVD speech compression.

The use of MMX technology reinforces AST's strategy of being quick-to-market with leading-edge technology. In January of this year, AST was the first PC manufacturer to begin volume shipments of Intel Pentium® Pro processor-based systems to the indirect channel.

Service and Support
AST backs all of its products with a host of multi-source support programs, including a toll-free "hot" line for users in the United States. Available as an option to commercial users is AST-LifeLine™, an innovative, interactive technical support system that incorporates Radish Communications System's VoiceView® integrated voice and data protocol with custom AST technical support software. Using an ordinary phone line, voice and data communications can be exchanged between the customer and PC support technician to quickly evaluate the situation and implement a "real time" solution or "fix" during a single call.

Info-FAX, a 24-hour local and international voice-automated fax-response product information source lets users download product information. Additionally, AST OnLine!, AST's own bulletin board service, as well as Compuserve® (GO:ASTFORUM), America Online® (KEYWORD:AST) and Prodigy® (JUMP:AST SUPPORT) online forums allow users to talk, meet, share ideas and answer questions with other AST users. Information about AST and its products can be found on the World Wide Web at http://www.ast.com.
