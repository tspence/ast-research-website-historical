AST® COMPUTER DELIVERS A REMEDY TO RISING COST OF PC MANAGEMENT WITH NEW BRAVO COMMERCIAL SYSTEMS AND INTEL'S LANDESK® CLIENT MANAGER 3.0 SOFTWARE

Desktop Management Interface Means Minimized System Downtime,
Reduced IS Support and ® Enhanced Desktop Manageability

IRVINE, CALIF., May 27, 1997 - Providing a cure to high PC support costs is something that users have come to expect from AST® Computer. And selected new Bravo commercial systems from AST Computer do just that. AST's new Bravo MS commercial desktops will come preloaded with Intel LANDesk® Client Manager 3.0 software. Merging the best of AST software innovation with the best of Intel hardware know-how has created an optimal business solution from which entrepreneurs and Fortune 500 users alike can benefit.

"By combining Bravo's leading-edge technology with enhanced desktop management capabilities from Intel's LANDesk, AST is helping to alleviate the headaches associated with the high costs of computing," stated Laura Raybin, director, brand marketing, AST Computer. "Small, medium and large business users alike can look forward to a heightened level of productivity, cost-effectiveness and manageability with the Bravo line of commercial desktops."

AST's use of LANDesk product technology extends the efforts of Intel's Wired for Management initiative to further reduce the costs of business computing without sacrificing the benefits of high-performance PC computing.

Desktop Management for Maximum Efficiency

Intel's LANDesk Client Manager software is Desktop Management Interface (DMI) compliant and enhances the management capabilities of PCs, thereby reducing the total cost of ownership associated with business computing. With greater ease of management through LANDesk Client Manager on AST's new Bravo systems, problems can be proactively handled to reduce system downtime and decrease the expense of labor associated with system/network management and support.

LANDesk Client Manager 3.0 manages all aspects of the computer to LAN connection, providing the user with the ability to achieve greater functionality and productivity. Specifically, the self-diagnostic capabilities maintain real-time PC health by predicting potential problems and component failure. LANDesk alerts users prior to failure, resulting in greatly reduced computer downtime and service needs. Ultimately, this improves employee productivity and reduces the need for costly support from either the IS staff or component/system vendor.

Critical desktop information is never more than a keystroke away with Bravo computers through LANDesk Client Manager. IS departments can easily service and track PC and network assets any time of day through the administrator and remote access functions. Plus, client installation and computer upgrades can be simplified with LANDesk's integration onto AST's Bravo systems.

"With its new Bravo MS computers, AST is offering valuable management capabilities to help businesses further reduce their computing costs," said Ed Ekstrom, vice president of Intel's Internet and Communications Group and general manager of the System Management Division. "These powerful Bravo systems with LANDesk Client Manager are a fine example of how business PCs can provide greater functionality and manageability for increased IS productivity, while simultaneously minimizing the amount of IS support required."

AST designed the Bravo systems with full network compatibility in mind. Through LANDesk Client Manager, the Bravo systems provide compatibility with leading connections to the LAN, enterprise management functions and network operating systems.

The new Bravo systems with Intel's LANDesk Client Manager 3.0 is scheduled to begin shipping in May.
