AST EMPOWERS ITS EMPLOYEES AND CUSTOMERS WITH WINDOWS® NT® CERTIFICATION PROGRAM

New Windows NT 4.0 Programs and Product Features Reinforce AST's Commitment of Providing Leading-Edge Corporate Business Tools

IRVINE, Calif., Oct. 16, 1996 - Leading the migration towards Windows NT and Pentium® Pro technology, AST Computer announced today that its employees have become "PowerPro" certified. Through the Certification Program, AST has taken the unusual step of training and certifying all AST employees, both technical and non-technical, on the benefits of Windows NT and Pentium Pro technology. This training program demonstrates AST's recognition of the corporate community's move towards 32-bit computing environments.

"AST is proud to be implementing a far-reaching training program that expands our employees' understanding of these new technologies," said Laura Raybin, AST's director of worldwide product marketing for commercial desktops. "The market is on the brink of a new wave of computing and we see this training as a way to plan for the future. The education of our employees proves that we have a strong understanding of the 32-bit environment and can anticipate the needs of the business community."

Your First Source for Information
Earlier this month, AST employees attended interactive training sessions to certify them in the Windows NT and Pentium Pro environments. These training sessions enabled AST employees to "talk the talk" of Pentium Pro and Windows NT power users. During these sessions, they learned the inner workings of the 32-bit environment and why this direction is so important to all AST employees, as well as the business community at large.

More than 2,700 AST employees in this nationwide training program received a combination of technical training and industry overviews, enabling them to better understand the operating system and its place in the market. With a Pentium Pro and Windows NT education under their belts, AST employees have the expertise to effectively offer the right solutions. The company encourages all potential PowerPro users to access AST as a first-source for 32-bit computing information.

AST Goes the Extra Mile
For those customers who are unsure if they are ready to move towards 32-bit computing applications, AST has developed additional programs to smoothly transition customers to this leading-edge technology. AST's Test Drive Program allows qualifying prospective customers to evaluate Bravo MS-T Pro Series systems at their own pace. This innovative program works to better familiarize customers with Windows NT 4.0 and Pentium Pro processor capabilities. The Test Drive Program runs concurrently with Intel's Seed Unit Program, of which AST is an active participant. The Seed Unit Program is another means for large corporations to test Pentium Pro technology within their own computing environment.

Meeting Today's Needs and Tomorrow's Challenges
"AST has noticed a dramatic growth among Pentium Pro users migrating to the Windows NT Workstation platform," Raybin explained. "End-users and resellers alike have questions about 32-bit environments, how to implement them, what features they need and what benefits to expect. With our unprecedented training and by providing the required product features, corporate users can feel confident that they have a business partner who can meet today's requirements as well as tomorrow's challenges."

AST Unlocks the Power
AST is making the commitment towards 32-bit computing applications both in product and programs. AST now ships all Bravo MS-T Pro 6180 and 6200 Pentium Pro-based commercial desktop computers with the Windows NT 4.0 operating system and the Intel® EtherExpress™ PRO/100B network-ready solution. These systems are aggressively priced with an estimated street price of $2,490 for the Bravo 6180 model and $3,036 for the Bravo 6200 model.

"We are delighted that AST is pre-installing Windows NT Workstation 4.0 on their Bravo MS-T Pro systems designed for large organizations," said Pat Fox, group manager, Desktop and Business Systems Division at Microsoft. "AST customers are the real winners as they experience the flexibility, reliability and performance of the Windows NT 32-bit architecture."

Windows NT 4.0 provides the Bravo MS-T Pro series with the highest level of system reliability and data protection for demanding environments such as transaction processing, graphics handling and other mission critical applications. It is the only network operating system that is multi-purpose, scales from the workgroup across to the enterprise and has built-in Internet and Intranet support.

The Intel EtherExpress PRO/100B networking card provides Bravo MS-T Pro series users a network-ready solution designed specifically for the Pentium Pro processor and the PCI bus. It dramatically improves total system performance and user productivity while maximizing throughput over standard Ethernet or Fast Ethernet networks. The EtherExpress' high-performance architecture minimizes CPU utilization by seamlessly migrating from 10Mbps to 100Mbps. This equips the Bravo MS-T Pro 6180 and 6200 systems with even more computing power to meet even the most demanding applications.
