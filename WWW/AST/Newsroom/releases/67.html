AST COMPUTER UNVEILS COMPREHENSIVE PROGRAM TO MOVE CORPORATE CUSTOMERS TO PENTIUM® PRO PROCESSORS AND WINDOWS NT® TECHNOLOGIES

AST Joins With IDC to Study Corporate Migration Patterns; Employs Unprecedented Tactics to Enable Smooth Transition to Advanced Technologies

IRVINE, Calif., November 5, 1996 - Industry-leading developer of high-end business desktop systems, AST Computer (NASDAQ:ASTA), today announced that findings in a new International Data Corporation (IDC) white paper, entitled The Successful Migration to Next Generation PCs, clearly support AST's aggressive deployment strategy for powerful Pentium Pro processor-based commercial desktop systems. The white paper, which was sponsored by AST, is phase one of a comprehensive program AST has designed to help its corporate customers transition to the 32-bit environment.

"AST has gone to great lengths to not only validate our future product direction, but also to help our corporate customers understand the benefits and challenges they face with the migration to the latest technologies," explained Dan Sheppard, vice president, global corporate marketing at AST. "In addition to the study with IDC, AST is conducting worldwide briefings on the research findings, educating all its employees on the benefits of Windows NT software and Pentium Pro processors, initiated a 'Test Drive' evaluation program and is also working with Intel® on their Seed Unit Program. These types of innovative programs demonstrate AST's commitment to our customers and reseller partners."

White Paper Findings
To confirm the migration pattern of multinational corporations toward Microsoft® Windows NT™ software and Intel® Pentium® Pro technologies, AST partnered with IDC to conduct a worldwide study and produce a white paper on the findings. IDC surveyed 1,500 medium and large corporations in the United States, Europe and Asia/Pacific.

Since the study addressed advanced Windows NT adoption, IDC focused on only those corporations who planned to have Windows NT running on at least ten percent of their total systems within the next two years.

IDC research found that there is a planned 150 percent increase in Windows NT usage from the fall of 1996 to the fall of 1997. Further, 50 percent of those surveyed stated that at least half of their PC purchases by the end of 1997 will be Pentium Pro processor-based. Given these factors, IDC predicts that 1998 will be the pivotal cross-over year when U.S. Pentium Pro processor-based PC sales exceed Pentium processor-based PC sales overall, and when Windows NT installations will surpass Windows® 95 installations on desktops in large corporations.

"The results of this survey reinforce our continued belief that there is a dramatic shift toward Windows NT software and Pentium Pro processor-based systems in the corporate enterprise," stated Chris Yalonis, director of consulting for personal systems, IDC. "We have discovered that Corporate America sees Windows NT as a robust, general-purpose computing operating system which is very relevant for general business applications, not just high-end vertical applications. The data reinforces IDC's outlook that migration to next-generation desktop systems is happening at a much faster rate than earlier anticipated."

Declining Advanced PC Costs Add Incentive for Migration
The study points out that one of the biggest barriers to NT and Pentium Pro processor adoption is cost of hardware. However, IDC also points out that the initial cost of advanced PCs continues to decline significantly, and cites AST systems as an industry example. IDC reports that AST's 1996 Bravo series of advanced desktop systems are about one-third lower when compared to the advanced desktop systems AST offered in late 1994. Furthermore, IDC reports that true PC cost-to-use factors (which are actually more than 90 percent related to training and support staffing) favor the reliability, security and overall performance of the NT software and Pentium Pro processor combination.

AST Matches IDC Recommendations
IDC explains that choosing the right vendor is as important as choosing the right PC. Supporting AST's aggressive pricing on its Bravo systems, IDC urges customers to look for the PC vendors that push the price performance ratios and offer the most complete value package. Also cited as very important is the level of technical support offered by the vendor as well as related costs.

"AST has taken the lead in service and support as well as implementing industry-leading pricing on our commercial desktop computers," commented Laura Raybin, AST's director of worldwide product marketing for commercial desktops and advanced systems. "In fact, AST continues to set new levels of industry excellence in service and support. Within the past year, AST has reduced the average call wait time from six minutes to one minute. And our spare parts fill rate has exceeded our goal of 95 percent - reaching a high of 96.5 percent."

AST Sets New Standard with Education Programs
To provide customers with the variety of programs they need to make an educated decision, AST has developed additional programs to help them move to 32-bit computing applications. AST's Test Drive Program allows qualifying prospective customers to evaluate Bravo MS-T Pro Series systems at their own pace. The Test Drive Program works concurrently with Intel's Seed Unit Program, of which AST is one of six select participants. Intel's program is designed to provide large corporations with the opportunity to evaluate Pentium Pro technology within their own environment. In addition to systems, AST is providing a warranty upgrade promotion, enhanced technical support options and easy to read information sheets.

AST's Windows NT Software and Pentium Pro Processor Solutions
AST has committed to meet this rapidly increasing demand for Windows NT software Pentium Pro processor-based systems with its powerful line of Bravo commercial desktops. Bravo MS-T 6180 and 6200 desktop minitowers are bundled with Windows NT Workstation 4.0 and feature powerful 180- and 200MHz Pentium Pro processors to take full advantage of 32-bit applications.

On its high-powered desktop Bravo systems, AST takes communications to new heights with its first-of-a kind network browsing, Web authoring software bundle - AST-IntraAccess. This type of leading-edge innovation puts AST ahead of the pack for corporate computing solutions. AST-IntraAccess provides commercial customers with a complete network browsing and Web authoring software bundle, and includes all of the software that is necessary to make sharing data via a company Intranet a breeze.

IDC Survey Background and White Paper Availability
The white paper entitled, The Successful Migration to Next Generation PCs is based on research performed by IDC in the summer of 1996. IDC surveyed 500 IT and department managers in medium and large U.S. corporations on their advanced desktop PC usage and plans.

Parallel research programs implemented in Europe and Asia were also performed to obtain a global market perspective from more than 1,500 worldwide business enterprises. Overall, the IDC survey was conducted in 12 countries and in nine languages worldwide. International survey results show that by late 1997 there is expected to be a 500 percent increase in Windows NT usage among European corporate users, and a 400 percent increase in Windows NT usage among Asian corporate users. The white paper can be obtained by calling (800) 447-0023, ext. 100.
