AST FIRST TO SHIP PENTIUM® PRO SYSTEMS
TO INDIRECT SALES CHANNEL

Quick to Market is Cornerstone of Turnaround Plan

IRVINE, Calif., Feb. 5, 1995 -- Representing a key strategy designed to increase performance in accord with its turnaround plan, AST Research (ASTA-NASDAQ) today announced it is the first manufacturer to deliver systems to the indirect sales channel incorporating the latest PC-based technologies from Intel and other manufacturers.

AST today became the first manufacturer to ship desktop systems in volume to computer resellers based on Intel's Pentium® Pro microprocessor, the most advanced computing engine available to date. In addition, AST is shipping one of the industry's most advanced remote diagnostics products, AST-LifeLine, that enables quicker technical support responses at lower costs for its Bravo and Premmia business desktop systems.

"Quick to market and AST will become synonymous, interchangeable terms," said Ian Diery, AST president and chief executive officer. "In addition to being the cornerstone of our turnaround plan, delivering leading-edge technologies in advance of our competitors strengthens our commitment to the indirect channel and enables our resellers to provide a competitive response to Compaq and IBM, as well as direct marketers like Dell and Gateway."

Bravo minitower systems based on Intel's Pentium Pro processor and Microsoft's 32-bit Windows NT™ operating system incorporate leading edge technologies and are well suited for processing-intensive environments including CAD, graphics, financial and statistical applications. Incorporating the Intel Pentium Pro processor into AST's Bravo line extends the award-winning line of AST products to support the complete range of currently-available Pentium and Pentium Pro processor platforms from Intel and results in an optimum solution for business users looking to maximize their PC investment.

The Bravo MS-T 6150 is one of AST's most advanced business desktop systems. Its aggressive street price of $4,300 enables it to be a viable, cost-effective alternative to implement many tasks traditionally performed on more costly systems. It offers a high level of expandability with five drive bays -- two internal 3.5-inch and three external 5.25-inch -- along with four 32-bit PCI slots and three 16-bit ISA slots. Other features include:

  • Enhanced performance from a 150 MHz Pentium Pro processor
  • Robust storage from hard drives up to 1.6 GB
  • Fast data access with 16 MB of memory, expandable to 128 MB with 256 KB secondary cache
  • Quick graphics and vibrant color from a PCI-based 64-bit ATI graphics controller with 2 MB of video RAM, expandable to 4 MB
  • Realistic stereo sound from 16-bit SoundBlaster®-compatible audio

AST LifeLine is an interactive technical support system that delivers the ultimate in customer support by significantly reducing the time and costs associated with resolving technical issues. It allows remote diagnosis and repair of business system problems by simultaneously exchanging voice and data over an ordinary phone line. AST PC technicians, as well as resellers, can use AST LifeLine to look at the user's system files and configurations in real time, while also discussing the issues surrounding the problem. This integrated approach enables quick fixes for many problems -- often in a single phone call.

"Products like AST LifeLine provide our customers with quick solutions to critical problems, and our new systems equipped with the latest technology provide strategic platforms for increased performance," added Diery.

Corporate Background

AST Research Inc., a member of the Fortune 500 list of America's largest industrial and service companies, is one of the world's leading personal computer manufacturers. The $2.468 billion company develops a broad spectrum of desktop, mobile and server PC products that are sold in more than 100 countries worldwide.
