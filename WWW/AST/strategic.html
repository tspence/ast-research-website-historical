Strategic Direction
“AST forms the linchpin in Samsung’s plan for defining, developing and producing the new technologies of the 21st century,” said S.T. Kim, AST president and chief executive officer and head of Samsung’s global PC operations.  “As a world leader in semiconductors, displays and other computer technologies, Samsung will use AST as the front runner for linking the various Samsung Electronics divisions together to leverage Samsung’s diversity and vision of being a total solutions provider. As a total solutions provider, Samsung will integrate products and services from the company’s numerous technology divisions  which include computers, semiconductors, consumer electronics, communications equipment and software, to deliver new and unique offerings for business and home users worldwide.

“We will witness a change in both our business and personal lifestyles as the digital revolution takes hold.  We believe that together, AST and Samsung are poised for tremendous growth in these areas,” said Kim.

AST’s vision is to be a leading information technology solutions provider, ensuring that its customers succeed in the ever-evolving world of information technology. The company’s mission is twofold: to be the most responsive company in the PC industry by enabling customers to achieve their goals and to surpass customer expectations for technology, timeliness, quality and service. AST’s long-term goals include becoming the industry’s leading information technology provider and being one of the world’s top five PC suppliers by 2005.  To accomplish this, the company’s short and medium-term activities involve aggressive product development and marketing targeted to both large corporations and small to medium-sized businesses. 

“We have created a new AST,” said Kim.  “Stability, a return to profitability and being the industry’s most responsive company are the goals for the remainder of this year and throughout 1998.  Once this is achieved, we will focus on growth by continuing to deliver better value to customers than our competitors. In addition, to satisfy volatile customer needs in a timely manner, we are reducing our order-to-delivery cycles as a result of more efficient manufacturing operations.  By the year 2000, we plan to proliferate our server business in the multimedia network environment and expand our business in the consumer sector with convergence products such as PC Theater.  From these efforts, we expect to regain market share and expand within a new emerging market as an industry leader.”

AST’s aggressive product development efforts will be supported by significantly increasing the company’s R&D investments.  Plans call for doubling the R&D budget by 1999 and quadrupling spending by 2001. In focusing on the commercial segment, AST will differentiate its business desktop systems to meet specific geographical and functional needs.  Future desktop designs will employ a modular design to support the company’s new build-to-order strategy.

For notebooks, AST will utilize Samsung’s vast resources in flat-panel displays, hard disk drives, memory and other components to produce high-end and mid-range systems with the latest and most popular features.

For servers, AST has significantly increased its activities to focus on application servers for the Internet and networked PCs.  The company will soon expand its server development team in order to enhance its development efforts and broaden the scope of the company’s product offerings.

Each of these products is backed by industry-leading service and support plans that are among the industry’s most responsive.  For example, calls to AST’s U.S. telephone support centers have had an average call pick up time of under one minute.  The company also offers the industry’s only overnight notebook PC replacement through an arrangement with Federal Express. AST backs all of its products with a host of multi-source support programs including a toll-free “hot” line for users in the United States.
