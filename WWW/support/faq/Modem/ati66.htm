Initialization Strings

Modems generally use a series of AT commands, called initialization strings, to provide a good connection to another modem. Initialization strings are personal, and your best bet will be to contact your Internet service provider (ISP) to help you develop the best string that meets your needs.

Listed below is a common initialization string for this modem:

AT&F&C1&D2

The following string will disable error correction and data compression:

AT&F&C1&D2/N0%C0

Note that /NO disables error correction, while %CO disables data compression.

Listed below are some other common strings for this modem:

CompuServe - ATS0=0Q0V1X4

Prodigy - AT%CW1S95=0

Spry Internet in a Box ver. 1.0 - ATS0=0E1Q0V1X4

Spry Internet in a Box ver. 2.0 - ATS0=0E1Q0V1X4&K3

Spry Internet Office - ATS0=0E1Q0V1X4&K3

Often ISPs will have specific settings for you to select within a communication applet. The selection you make within these applets automatically configures the appropriate initialization string for you to use. For most communication applets you can select an ATI 600 Series modem, "Generic 28,800", or "Hayes Compatible 28,800."
