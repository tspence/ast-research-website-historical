Win-Win Business Negotiations

In today's complex business environment, a profitable relationship is developed when both parties are satisfied with the value of the agreement. The difference between success and failure is negotiation. You can create a win-win situation for your company and your business partners. Good agreements help you reach and exceed your goals. The skills taught in this program are necessary for anyone who must buy, sell, negotiate, persuade, motivate, or manage.

Seminar objectives:

  • Increase satisfaction through win-win settlements.
  • Avoid deadlocks; encourage the closing of profitable business deals.
  • Understand how the new research on EQ, self-esteem, and self-responsibility impact the modern business negotiator.
  • Develop compelling reasons and tactics to persuade the other side into win-win settlements.
  • Use effective planning skills in preparation for the negotiation, and analyze both the side's perspectives.
  • Learn practical techniques for creating a win-win environment within your own company.
  • Increase profits and satisfaction through the use of ethical negotiating skills.

To be successful in today's business climate, you must do more than establish good working relationships. You must be able to motivate people to exercise their decisions in your favor. You must be able to directly impact the people who can implement change and get results. Become proficient at developing the skills of persuasion and influence in group and one-on-one interactions. The skills taught in this program are necessary for anyone who must sell, negotiate, persuade, inform or represent.

Seminar objectives:

  • Understand the Power of Influence and influencing without power. This self-empowerment philosophy is critical in business organizations today.
  • Develop the verbal persuasive skills to compose convincing reasons that get people to act.
  • Understand the importance of EQ vs. IQ and how it impacts the decision process.
  • Develop a network of people who trust and support you, and how to effectively utilize them.
  • Enhance your perceived power in groups and one-on-one negotiations.
  • Build collaborative alliances with people by addressing the emotional and logical reasons that compel them to action.
