Value Selling Commodity Products

In today’s commodity market it is becoming more difficult to differentiate your products and services to your customers. AST has developed a workshop called “How To Value Sell Commodity Products” which delivers techniques on how to help you “win”
more business.

AST’s Sales Training Manager, Dave Cobb has years of experience selling, training and working with the top 15 consulting firms in the US as well as high tech manufacturers. The value of his experience and knowledge has been packed into the Value Selling Commodity Products workshop and made available to you and your sales reps as an Ovation! Gold Reseller. As part of the workshop, each attendee will walk away with the skills necessary to win more profitable business. Program materials are provided to each attendee.

Workshop Overview

Account Planning Strategies

This segment covers the differences and plans of attack that consulting firms do when they do their account planning, which is different from vendors.

Consulting Firms Secrets to Winning Deals

Consulting firms never seem to lose. They are always able somehow to wedge themselves between vendors and the decision-makers. This segment uncovers the secrets consulting firms have effectively used for years to get to decision-makers and how you can effectively use these techniques.

Winning with Internal Selling Slides

This segment will uncover how to turn around and make a sale from an opportunity that has no budget. Many sales people would naturally walk away from an opportunity with no budget.

How to Stop a Competitive Purchase

This segment uncovers many of the secrets consultants have used for years to stop competitive deals and ways to get your deal repositioned.

How to Structure a Winning Proposal

Effective techniques that consultants have used for years that put your proposal at the top of the pile and put you into a winning position over your competition.

How to Structure a Winning Presentation

This segment uncovers hidden secrets consultants have used for years on how to beat out competition on presentations.
